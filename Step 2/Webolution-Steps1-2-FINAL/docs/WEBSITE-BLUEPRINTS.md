# PART 1: COACHES & BERATER

## 1.1 Who Visits a Coach's Website?

Three visitor types, in order of frequency:

**Visitor A: The Searcher (60%)**
Googled "Business Coach München" or "Karrierecoaching." They have a problem (stuck in career, team conflict, life transition) and are looking for someone who can help. They're comparing 3-5 coaches. They decide in 30 seconds whether this coach COULD be the right one. What they need: Immediately understand what this coach does, for whom, and whether it feels right. Then: easy way to book a first conversation.

**Visitor B: The Referred (30%)**
Someone recommended this coach. They already have a name. They're on the website to VALIDATE the recommendation. "My friend said Sandra is great – let me check her website." What they need: Confirmation that this coach is professional, experienced, and trustworthy. Social proof matters enormously (testimonials, credentials, results).

**Visitor C: The Researcher (10%)**
HR manager looking for an executive coach for their team. Or a journalist researching coaches for an article. Or another coach checking out the competition. What they need: Professional presentation, clear methodology, credentials, possibly corporate references.

## 1.2 The Perfect Coach Website: Page by Page

### Homepage (Single most important page – 80% of visitors never go further)

**Above the fold (first viewport, no scrolling):**
- Hero photo: REAL photo of the coach. Not stock. Not AI. Authentic, warm, professional. Ideally: coaching situation (1:1 conversation) or a confident portrait in a real environment (office, nature, urban). NOT: corporate headshot on white background (too cold), NOT: stock photo of someone else.
- Headline: ONE sentence that answers: "What do you do and for whom?" Not generic ("Coaching für Ihr Potential") but specific ("Business-Coaching für Frauen in Führungspositionen" or "Karriere-Neustart nach dem Burnout"). The more specific, the better.
- Subline: One sentence that adds the HOW or the RESULT. "In 12 Wochen von der Überforderung zur klaren Strategie." or "Systemisches Coaching mit Fokus auf nachhaltige Veränderung."
- Primary CTA: "Kennenlerngespräch buchen" or "Kostenloses Erstgespräch vereinbaren." Button, not link. High contrast color. ABOVE THE FOLD – the visitor must see it without scrolling.
- Trust indicator (small, not dominant): "Seit 2015" or "ICF-zertifiziert" or "4.9 ★ bei 23 Bewertungen" – one line that builds instant credibility.

**Below the fold (scrolling sections, in this order):**

Section 1: "What I Help With" (Problem-focused, not service-list)
NOT a bullet list of services. Instead: 2-3 short paragraphs that describe the PROBLEMS the target audience faces. "Sie stehen vor einer Entscheidung die Ihr ganzes Berufsleben verändert." The visitor should think: "That's exactly me." Then: "In meinem Coaching arbeiten wir an [specific approach]." Each problem-block can have a small icon but text is king.

Section 2: "About Me" (Authentic, not CV)
NOT a career timeline. Instead: a personal story that explains WHY this person became a coach. What drives them. What they believe in. Written in first person ("Ich" not "Sandra K."). 150-300 words. Authentic photo (different from hero – maybe more relaxed, showing personality). Credentials listed AFTER the story, not as the main content: certifications, memberships, years of experience, education – as a compact list or badge row.

Section 3: Social Proof
If testimonials available: 2-3 client quotes with first name, context (if possible). "Sandra hat mir geholfen, den Mut für den Jobwechsel zu finden. – Michaela, Marketing-Leiterin"
If Google Reviews available: embedded widget or styled quotes with Google attribution.
If neither: "Seit [year]" badge + certification logos + "X+ Klienten begleitet" (only if verifiable).

Section 4: "How It Works" (Process transparency)
3 steps, visualized simply: 1) Kennenlerngespräch (kostenlos, 30 Min) → 2) Individuelles Coaching-Programm → 3) Nachhaltige Veränderung. Removes the mystery. Shows the visitor exactly what happens if they reach out.

Section 5: Secondary CTA
Full-width colored section. "Bereit für den nächsten Schritt?" + CTA button + optional: phone number and email for those who prefer not to book online.

**Footer:**
Contact info (email, phone, address if applicable), links to Impressum + Datenschutz, social media icons (if active profiles exist), optional: newsletter signup.

### About Page (for coach-authority template, separate page)
Extended version of the About section. Full biography. Professional photo(s). Philosophy/approach in detail. Qualifications listed formally. Publications, talks, media appearances if any.

### Services/Angebote Page
Each service/offering as its own section: name, who it's for, what it includes, duration, optionally price. CTA per service ("Mehr erfahren" or "Gespräch vereinbaren").

### Blog/Podcast/Resources Page (if content exists)
Clean grid of articles/episodes. Each with: title, date, short excerpt, featured image. Establishes authority and helps SEO.

### Contact Page
Large, friendly form: Name, Email, Phone (optional), "Wie kann ich Ihnen helfen?" (textarea). Map if physical location exists. Direct contact info repeated. Calendly embed or link for direct booking.

## 1.3 Visual Design Direction: Coaches

**Overall feel:** Warm, professional, personal. Not corporate. Not clinical. The visitor should feel: "This person is approachable AND competent."

**Color palette:**
- Primary: Warm tones – deep teal, warm navy, forest green, terracotta, or burgundy. NOT bright/aggressive colors.
- Accent: Contrasting but harmonious – gold/amber for warmth, coral for energy, sage for calm.
- Background: Off-white or very light warm grey. NOT pure white (too clinical) or dark (too corporate).
- Text: Dark grey (#2D2D44) not pure black. Softer, more readable.

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Modern serif (for authority + warmth) or rounded sans-serif (for approachability). Examples: Playfair Display, Lora, DM Serif Display, or: Nunito, Outfit, Plus Jakarta Sans.
- Body: Clean sans-serif, excellent readability. Inter, Source Sans 3, DM Sans. Size: 18px minimum for body text.
- Avoid: Geometric sans-serifs that feel cold (Futura, Montserrat). Avoid: script/handwriting fonts (unprofessional).

**Imagery:**
- Priority 1: Real photos of the coach (authentic, not over-retouched)
- Priority 2: Real photos of coaching environment (office, meeting room)
- Priority 3: Lifestyle photography that matches the target audience (professional women for executive coaching, nature/calm for life coaching)
- NEVER: Generic business stock photos (handshake, team high-five, woman-staring-at-laptop)
- NEVER: AI-generated portraits
- Style: Natural light, warm tones, shallow depth of field, real environments

**Layout principles:**
- Generous whitespace – the website should BREATHE
- One idea per viewport height (section)
- Text blocks max 65 characters per line for readability
- Images large (not thumbnails) – they set the emotional tone
- Mobile: everything stacks vertically, images full-width, text readable without zoom

**Micro-interactions:**
- Sections fade in on scroll (subtle, 400ms, once)
- Buttons: slight scale + shadow on hover
- Navigation: smooth scroll to sections (single-page) or instant transitions (multi-page)
- NO: parallax scrolling (dated), NO: auto-playing videos, NO: pop-ups

## 1.4 Conversion Elements: Coaches

**Primary CTA:** "Kennenlerngespräch buchen" – appears: hero section, after services, end of page. Always same text for consistency. Links to: Calendly embed, contact form, or phone number.

**Trust builders:**
- Certification badges (ICF, dvct, DBVC) in a row
- "Seit [year]" – years of experience as number
- Google rating if >4.0
- Client testimonials with first name and context
- "Kostenlos und unverbindlich" near the CTA to reduce fear

**Urgency (honest, not fake):**
- "Begrenzte Plätze pro Monat" (if true – many coaches do limit their client count)
- "Nächster freier Termin: [date]" (if Calendly integration shows availability)
- NO fake countdown timers, NO "nur noch 3 Plätze"

## 1.5 What Makes a Coach Website Go From "Good" to "Incredible"

The difference between a 7/10 website and a 10/10:

**Personality.** The website doesn't feel like a template. It feels like you're meeting the coach before meeting them. Their voice is in the text. Their face is in the photos. Their philosophy is in the structure. You know EXACTLY what kind of person they are after 30 seconds.

**Specificity.** Not "Coaching für alle Lebensbereiche" but "Ich helfe Führungskräften die nachts nicht mehr schlafen können." The more specific, the more the right visitor thinks "this is for ME."

**One clear action.** Not 5 buttons going to different places. ONE path: read → feel connection → book call. Everything on the page serves this one journey.

**Speed.** The website loads in 1.5 seconds. The old website loaded in 8. The visitor FEELS the difference even if they don't consciously notice it.

**Mobile perfection.** 70% of visitors come from phones. The website on mobile isn't a shrunk desktop version – it's designed mobile-first. Thumb-friendly buttons, readable text, images that fit the screen.

# PART 2: FRISEURE & SALONS

## 2.1 Who Visits a Salon's Website?

**Visitor A: The Booker (50%)**
Wants to book an appointment. Maybe they're already a customer, maybe they found the salon on Google. They want: prices, available times, and a BOOK button. Speed is everything – if they can't book in 60 seconds, they go to the next salon.

**Visitor B: The Chooser (35%)**
Looking for a NEW salon. Googled "Friseur in der Nähe" or saw the salon on Instagram. They're comparing 3-5 salons. What convinces them: photos of real work (haircuts, colors, styling), the overall vibe of the salon (modern? classic? edgy?), prices (transparent), and reviews. The website is their FIRST IMPRESSION of the salon.

**Visitor C: The Info-Seeker (15%)**
Looking for specific info: opening hours (am I too late?), address (where exactly?), phone number (quick question). They need this info in 5 seconds. If it's buried in a PDF or not on the homepage: they leave.

## 2.2 The Perfect Salon Website: Page by Page

### Homepage

**Above the fold:**
- Hero: STUNNING photo of the salon interior or a beautiful hair result. Full-bleed (edge to edge), high quality, well-lit. This is the #1 conversion element for salons – the photo SELLS. If no good photo exists: moody, stylish background with text overlay (dark gradient + white text).
- Salon name: prominent but not larger than the photo
- Tagline: one line that communicates the vibe. "Dein Salon für Farbe und Stil in Kreuzberg" or "Seit 1998 – Friseurhandwerk mit Leidenschaft"
- Primary CTA: "Termin buchen" – big button, impossible to miss. This must be the MOST prominent element after the photo.
- Opening hours: visible in the hero or immediately below. Visitors need this FIRST.

**Below the fold:**

Section 1: Gallery (THE key section for salons)
Grid of 6-12 photos. Priority: own work (before/after, styling results) > salon interior > team at work > product shots. Each image should be high quality and show the salon's STYLE. Instagram-aesthetic: square or consistent aspect ratio, warm lighting, professional quality.
NOT: random phone photos, blurry shots, photos from 2015 with outdated hairstyles.
If Instagram exists with good photos: embed the feed or curate the best photos from there.

Section 2: Services & Prices
Clean, scannable price list. Categories: Damen, Herren, Kinder, Farbe, Styling, Pflege. Each with price or price range. No PDF download – the prices must be ON the page, not in a file.
Format: clear table or structured list. Not a wall of text.
If prices aren't public ("auf Anfrage"): list the services without prices + CTA "Preise erfragen."

Section 3: Team
Each stylist: name, photo (real, authentic – them at work or a friendly portrait), specialty ("Spezialisiert auf Balayage und Naturfarben"), optionally: short personal note.
For barbershops: first names + photos are enough. Keep it casual.
Team section builds CONNECTION – the visitor knows who will do their hair before they walk in.

Section 4: Reviews/Social Proof
Google Reviews widget or styled quotes. Salon's Google rating prominently displayed. "4.8 ★ aus 124 Bewertungen."
If Instagram is strong: "Folge uns auf Instagram" with follower count and 3-4 recent post thumbnails.

Section 5: Location & Hours
Embedded map (OpenStreetMap for privacy). Full address. Opening hours as clean table (Mo-Fr, Sa, So). Phone number (clickable on mobile). Public transport hints if urban.

Section 6: Booking CTA (full-width)
"Jetzt Termin vereinbaren" – booking tool embed (Treatwell, Booksy, Shore, or Calendly) OR phone number with "Telefonisch buchen: [number]" OR contact form.

**Footer:**
Address, phone, email, opening hours (repeated for quick access), Instagram link, Impressum + Datenschutz.

### Gallery Page (separate, for salons with lots of photos)
Masonry grid or carousel of all photos. Filterable by category: Farbe, Schnitt, Styling, Salon. Each photo zoomable.

### Team Page (separate, for larger salons)
Extended profiles per stylist. Specialties, experience, personal touch.

### Prices Page (separate, for salons with extensive service menus)
Fully structured price list. Anchor navigation to categories.

## 2.3 Visual Design Direction: Salons

**Overall feel:** The website should feel like the salon. If the salon is edgy and modern → dark, high-contrast, bold typography. If it's warm and welcoming → light, soft colors, friendly type. If it's a barbershop → dark/masculine, strong typography, vintage touches.

**Three design modes based on salon type:**

**Mode A: Modern/Premium Salon**
- Colors: Blush pink + charcoal, cream + gold, sage + white, mauve + dark navy
- Typography: Elegant sans-serif (Outfit, Poppins) or modern serif (Cormorant Garamond)
- Imagery: Bright, airy, Instagram-quality. White backgrounds. Soft shadows
- Layout: Lots of whitespace. Gallery-centric. Large photos

**Mode B: Classic/Traditional Salon**
- Colors: Warm beige + brown, ivory + forest green, cream + burgundy
- Typography: Classic serif (Playfair Display) + clean sans-serif body
- Imagery: Warm, traditional. Real salon photos with warm lighting
- Layout: Traditional structure. Text-image balance

**Mode C: Barbershop/Edgy**
- Colors: Black + gold, dark charcoal + amber, dark green + cream
- Typography: Strong condensed sans-serif (Bebas Neue, Oswald) or vintage-inspired (Playfair Display bold)
- Imagery: Dark, moody, high-contrast. Black and white photos with color accents
- Layout: Full-bleed images. Minimal text. Bold headlines

**Salon mode is detected from:** brand colors (dark = edgy, pastel = modern, warm = classic), salon name keywords (Barber/Barbier = barbershop), Google Business category, Instagram aesthetic analysis.

**Imagery:**
- Priority 1: Photos of actual hair work (results, before/after)
- Priority 2: Salon interior photos (the space)
- Priority 3: Team at work photos
- Priority 4: Product/tool close-ups
- NEVER: Generic stock photos of models with perfect hair (immediately recognizable as fake)
- NEVER: Low-resolution photos from 2015
- Fallback if no photos: stylized text-only sections with strong typography + NanoBanana ambient salon shots (tools, products, interior – NOT people)

**Mobile-critical elements:**
- "Anrufen" button (click-to-call) always visible on mobile
- "Route planen" button (opens Maps with directions)
- Booking CTA sticky at bottom of screen on mobile
- Gallery scrollable horizontally (not stacked vertically – takes too much space)

## 2.4 Conversion Elements: Salons

**Primary CTA:** "Termin buchen" – everywhere. Hero, after gallery, after prices, floating on mobile. Links to: Treatwell/Booksy embed, phone number, or booking form.

**Trust builders:**
- Google rating + review count prominently displayed
- Instagram follower count (if >500)
- "Seit [year]" for established salons
- Hygiene/quality badges if applicable
- Treatwell/Booksy rating if present

**Info that MUST be instantly findable:**
- Opening hours (saves phone calls)
- Address + map (saves "where are you?" calls)
- Price list (saves "how much does...?" calls)
- Phone number (for those who prefer calling)
Every piece of info that saves the salon a phone call = value.

## 2.5 What Makes a Salon Website Go From "Good" to "Incredible"

**The photos.** A salon website WITH stunning photos of real work is 10x more compelling than one without. The single biggest quality lever is: does the salon have good photos? If yes: the website will be amazing. If no: the website will be decent but not impressive. This means: Instagram photos are GOLD. Extracting them from Instagram may be the single most impactful thing we can do.

**The booking flow.** From "I want a haircut" to "appointment booked" in under 60 seconds. If the salon uses Treatwell/Booksy: embed the widget. If not: prominent phone number + opening hours so the visitor can call. The fewer clicks to an appointment, the better.

**The vibe match.** A hipster barbershop website should not look like a suburban family salon website. The design must match the salon's identity. This is where the 3 design modes (modern/classic/edgy) are critical. A mismatched vibe is worse than a generic one.

**The price transparency.** Salons that show their prices online convert better than those that don't. "Damen Waschen + Schneiden + Föhnen: ab €45" – no surprise at the register. If the salon doesn't publish prices: at minimum show price ranges.

# PART 3: CROSS-INDUSTRY PRINCIPLES

## 3.1 Universal Quality Standards (All Industries)

Every generated website, regardless of industry, must meet these:

**Performance:**
- Lighthouse Performance: >90
- Largest Contentful Paint: <2.5s on 4G
- Cumulative Layout Shift: <0.1
- Total page weight: <1.5MB (including images)
- Time to Interactive: <3.5s

**Accessibility (BFSG-mandatory):**
- All images have meaningful alt text (not "image1.jpg")
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible
- Color contrast ratio ≥4.5:1 for text (even with brand colors – override if needed)
- Form fields have proper labels
- Heading hierarchy (h1→h2→h3, no skipping)
- Focus indicators visible

**SEO:**
- Unique title tag per page (<60 chars, includes business name + city + service)
- Meta description per page (<155 chars, includes CTA)
- H1 on every page (only one)
- Schema.org markup (industry-specific: LocalBusiness, Attorney, Physician, HairSalon, etc.)
- Canonical URL set
- Sitemap.xml generated
- robots.txt configured

**Legal (Germany):**
- Impressum link in footer (accessible from every page)
- Datenschutzerklärung link in footer
- No cookie-setting external services (self-hosted fonts, OpenStreetMap, no Google Analytics)
- If forms: data processing notice near submit button

**Content truth:**
- Every fact has a source reference (not shown to visitor, stored in CMS/DB)
- No value judgments ("erfahren", "kompetent", "führend") unless directly quoted from a review
- No invented statistics
- Prices only shown if confirmed current (within 30 days)
- "Stand: [month/year]" indicator somewhere on the page

## 3.2 The Quality Gap That Sells

The generated website must be SO MUCH BETTER than the old one that the difference is OBVIOUS within 3 seconds of looking at the before/after comparison. This means:

| Dimension | Old Website (typical) | New Website (our standard) |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| First impression | "Looks outdated" | "Looks professional and modern" |
| Mobile | Broken or barely usable | Perfect, designed mobile-first |
| Speed | 5-10 seconds load | <2 seconds load |
| CTA | Hidden or missing | Prominent, clear, above the fold |
| Social proof | None | Google rating + testimonials + trust badges |
| Photos | Stock or none | Real (from Instagram/website) or curated premium |
| Content | Generic or sparse | Specific, benefit-oriented, authentic |
| Navigation | Confusing or broken | Clear, simple, 3-5 items max |
| Contact | Email link buried | Click-to-call, booking widget, prominent form |
| SEO | No meta tags, no schema | Full optimization, local SEO, schema markup |
| Legal | Missing or wrong Impressum | Complete, DSGVO-konform |

If the new website doesn't clearly win on at least 8 of these 11 dimensions: DO NOT deploy it as a preview. The comparison won't be convincing enough.

# PART 4: FUTURE INDUSTRIES (Skeleton)

When adding Steuerberater, Anwälte, Handwerker, Ärzte: create the same blueprint structure:

1. Who visits? (3 visitor types with needs)
2. Page-by-page structure (homepage above/below fold + subpages)
3. Visual design direction (colors, typography, imagery, layout)
4. Conversion elements (CTA, trust builders, urgency)
5. What makes it go from good to incredible
6. 5 reference websites

Quick notes for future industries:

**Anwälte:** Visitor needs: "Can I trust this person with my problem?" → authority + empathy balance. Visual: serious but not cold. Navy + gold. Photography: professional portraits, office environment. Key conversion: "Erstberatung vereinbaren." Key challenge: must feel trustworthy without feeling arrogant.

**Steuerberater:** Visitor needs: "Will this person make my tax life easier?" → competence + approachability. Visual: clean, organized (mirrors what they do). Blue/green + white. Key conversion: "Beratungstermin buchen." Key challenge: making a "boring" topic feel human and approachable.

**Handwerker:** Visitor needs: "Can this person do the job? Are they available?" → project photos + quick contact. Visual: strong, practical, not fancy. Dark colors + craft photography. Key conversion: "Angebot anfordern" or direct phone. Key challenge: most visitors want to CALL, not fill out forms. Phone number must be huge.

**Ärzte:** Visitor needs: "Is this the right doctor? Can I get an appointment?" → credentials + availability. Visual: clean, calming, medical-professional. Blue/white/green. Key conversion: "Termin vereinbaren" (Doctolib/Jameda embed). Key challenge: HWG legal restrictions on what can be said.
